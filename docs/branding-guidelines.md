# DeepSafe Brand Guidelines

## 1. Brand Essence

**Brand Name:** DeepSafe  
**Tagline:** Revolutionizing video call security against deepfakes

**Mission**  
Protect real human trust in digital meetings by detecting and stopping deepfake impersonation in real time.

**Brand Personality**
- Trustworthy & authoritative (security-first)
- Calm under pressure (no alarmism)
- Modern, technical, precise
- Invisible but powerful (hands-off protection)

**Primary Audience**  
CSOs, CISOs, Security & IT leaders at financial institutions and regulated enterprises.

---

## 2. Logo Usage (High-Level)

- Prefer the wordmark "DeepSafe" with high contrast
- Rounded geometry; avoid playful or sharp corners
- Minimum clear space = height of the "D" in DeepSafe
- Do not apply gradients *inside* the logo
- Logo may sit on gradient backgrounds

---

## 6. Gradient System (Core Brand Element)

Gradients are a **primary visual language** for DeepSafe and should appear across backgrounds, panels, charts, modals, hero sections, and dashboards.

Gradients are built from **black → navy → layered blues**. They are subtle, professional, and calm—never playful or neon.

---

---

### 6.6 Gradient Usage Rules

- Prefer **vertical or diagonal** gradients
- Maximum **3 color stops**
- Avoid mixing warm & cool gradients in the same component
- Gradients replace borders wherever possible
- Text overlays must meet **WCAG AA** contrast

> **Brand Principle:** If flat color feels sufficient, the gradient is missing.

## 7. Typography

### Primary Typeface (Product & UI)
**Inter**  
Weights: 400 / 500 / 600 / 700  
Usage: dashboards, alerts, tables, buttons

### Secondary Typeface (Marketing & Headings)
**Space Grotesk**  
Weights: 500 / 700  
Usage: pitch decks, landing pages, headlines

### Monospace (Optional)
**JetBrains Mono**  
Usage: logs, IDs, technical data

---

## 8. Iconography

- Line-based icons, 2px stroke
- Rounded terminals
- Filled icons reserved for critical alerts
- Avoid illustrative or playful styles

---

## 9. Voice & Tone

**Do**
- Be factual, calm, and precise
- Emphasize verification and protection
- Use security-first language

**Avoid**
- Sensational or fear-based phrasing
- Casual tone or humor
- Excessive punctuation

---

## 10. Accessibility

- WCAG AA minimum contrast (4.5:1)
- Never rely on color alone for alerts
- Dark mode treated as first-class experience

---

## 11. Brand North Star

**If DeepSafe disappeared, security teams should feel exposed.**
